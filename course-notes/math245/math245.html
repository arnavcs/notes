Example Definition Results

Affine Spaces

Let \(W\) be a vector space over a field \(\mathbb{F}\). An affine space in \(W\) is a set of the form \(P = p + U = \{ p + u | u \in U \}\) where \(p \in W\) and \(U \subseteq W\) is a subspace. As we see below, \(U\) is uniquely determined, so we say that it is the vector space associated to \(P\), and that \(P\) is the affine space through \(p\) with associated vector space \(U\).

Relation on Representatives of Affine Spaces

Let \(W\) be a vector space over a field \(\mathbb{F}\). Let \(p, q \in W\) and \(U, V \subseteq W\) be subspaces. If \(P = p + U\) and \(Q = q + V\), then

  1. \((P \subseteq Q) \Leftrightarrow (p \in Q \wedge U \subseteq V)\)

  2. \((P = Q) \Leftrightarrow (p \in Q \wedge U = V)\)

Additionally note that we have \(p \in Q \Leftrightarrow p - q \in V\).

Dimension of an Affine Space

Let \(P\) be the affine space throguh \(p\) with associated vector space \(U\). We say that

\(\displaystyle \dim P := \dim U\)

In specific cases, we give names to the forms created by the affine spaces.

\(\dim P\) 0 1 2 \(n - 1\)
\(P\) Point Line Plane Hyperplane

Note that when \(\dim P = 0\), we really have a set containing a point, not a point.

Quotient Space

Let \(W\) be a vector space over a field \(\mathbb{F}\), and \(V \subseteq W\) be a subspace. The qutient space \({}^W /_V := \{ w + V : w \in W \}\) is the saet containing all affine spaces associated with \(V\).

Codimension of a Subspace

Let \(W\) be a vector space over a field \(\mathbb{F}\), and \(V \subseteq W\) be a subspace. We say the codimension of \(V\) in \(W\) is \(\operatorname{codim}_W (V) := \dim \left( {}^W /_V \right)\).

Note that \(\operatorname{codim}_W (V) = 1 \Leftrightarrow V \text{ is a hyperplane}\).
